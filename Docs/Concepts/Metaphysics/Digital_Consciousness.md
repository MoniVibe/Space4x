**Status:** Concept Design
**Category:** Core Metaphysics Framework
**Shareability:** `game-specific-variant`
**Last Updated:** 2025-12-07

## Overview

The **Digital Consciousness System** is Space4X's implementation of the soul framework, treating consciousness as transferable data that can be uploaded, backed up, copied, and transferred between bodies (organic, synthetic, or digital). This Altered Carbon-inspired system enables immortality through resleeving, pilot-mech integration, and raises philosophical questions about identity and the nature of consciousness.

**Core Differences from Godgame:**
- **Technology-Based:** Cortical stacks, consciousness upload, digital storage (vs magic rituals)
- **Copy/Backup:** Souls can be duplicated (philosophical dilemma: which is "real"?)
- **Instant Transfer:** Stack insertion vs long rituals
- **Data Corruption:** Digital degradation vs soul fragmentation
- **Corporate Control:** Soul ownership, licensing, theft (vs divine/moral law)

**Integration Points:**
- **Altered Carbon Style:** Cortical stacks store consciousness, resleeving into new bodies
- **Pilot Integration:** Consciousness merges with mech/ship for enhanced control
- **AI vs Souled:** Distinction between programmed AI and uploaded human consciousness
- **Soul Trafficking:** Black market for unique personalities, illegal cloning
- **Backup/Restore:** Consciousness insurance, legal/ethical implications

## Cortical Stack (Soul Storage Device)

### Physical Device
**Description:** Coin-sized cybernetic implant at base of skull

**Properties:**
- **Capacity:** Stores complete consciousness (memories, personality, skills)
- **Durability:** Survives body death, extreme damage (military-grade)
- **Encryption:** Unique identity lock (hacking possible but illegal)
- **Lifespan:** 200+ years before degradation
- **Size:** 2cm diameter × 0.5cm thick

**Implantation:**
- Age: Birth or early childhood (standard practice)
- Procedure: 2-hour surgery, 95% success rate
- Cost: 5,000 credits (subsidized) to 50,000 credits (military-grade)
- Recovery: 1 week
- Rejection: 2% rate (immunosuppressants required)

**Backup Frequency:**
- Continuous: Real-time upload (wealthy, cloud-synced)
- Daily: Standard backup (middle class)
- Weekly: Budget backup (lower class)
- Never: Uninsured (stack-only backup, no cloud)

### Stack Extraction
**After Death:**
- Window: 48 hours (brain decay limits extraction)
- Procedure: 30-minute surgical extraction
- Success: 98% (if within window)
- Failure: Stack damaged, consciousness lost

**Extraction Services:**
- Legal: Hospitals, morgues, insurance companies
- Illegal: Stack thieves, black market harvesters
- Military: Emergency battlefield extraction (combat medics)

## Resleeving (Consciousness Transfer)

### Body Types

#### 1. Clone Bodies (Genetically Identical)
**Properties:**
- **Compatibility:** 100% (perfect genetic match)
- **Cost:** 500,000+ credits (extremely expensive)
- **Growth Time:** 18 months (accelerated growth tanks)
- **Advantages:** No rejection, original appearance, peak physical condition
- **Disadvantages:** Expensive, time-consuming, ethical concerns

**Example:**
```
Billionaire's Clone:
- Age: 25 (peak physical condition, grown from DNA sample)
- Compatibility: 100%
- Resleeving Time: 5 minutes (stack insertion)
- Recovery: 1 day (neural integration)
- Cost: 2,000,000 credits
- Lifespan: 100+ years (before next resleeve)
```

#### 2. Donor Bodies (Organic, Non-Matching)
**Properties:**
- **Compatibility:** 40-80% (depends on genetic similarity)
- **Cost:** 10,000-100,000 credits (market price)
- **Availability:** High (death row inmates, voluntary donations, black market)
- **Advantages:** Fast availability, cheaper than clones
- **Disadvantages:** Rejection symptoms, different appearance, may be damaged

**Rejection Symptoms:**
```
Compatibility 80-90%: Mild disorientation (1-2 days)
Compatibility 60-80%: Moderate symptoms (nausea, dizziness, 1 week)
Compatibility 40-60%: Severe rejection (vomiting, pain, 2-4 weeks, may fail)
Compatibility <40%: Critical rejection (50% death rate, requires intensive care)
```

#### 3. Synthetic Bodies (Artificial)
**Properties:**
- **Compatibility:** 70% (artificial nervous system interface)
- **Cost:** 50,000-500,000 credits (depends on quality)
- **Advantages:** No organic diseases, enhanced strength/durability, upgradeable
- **Disadvantages:** Less tactile sensation, uncanny valley, social stigma

**Example:**
```
Combat Synthetic:
- Material: Titanium endoskeleton, synthetic muscle fibers
- Stats: +100 STR, +80 AGI, +50 END (vs organic baseline)
- Durability: 5× human durability, self-repair nanites
- Sensation: 50% tactile sensitivity (eating, sex less pleasurable)
- Cost: 300,000 credits
- Lifespan: 50 years before major maintenance
```

#### 4. Digital Existence (AI Core)
**Properties:**
- **Compatibility:** N/A (no physical body)
- **Cost:** 100,000+ credits (server maintenance)
- **Advantages:** Immortal (no body aging), instant transfer, no physical vulnerabilities
- **Disadvantages:** No physical sensation, social isolation, existential crisis

**Digital Life:**
- **Existence:** Pure consciousness in virtual reality
- **Interaction:** Holographic projectors, VR interfaces, robotic avatars
- **Rights:** Debated (are digital consciousnesses "alive"?)
- **Purpose:** Research, data analysis, virtual world habitation

## Consciousness Upload Process

### Initial Upload (Organic → Digital)
**Procedure:**
1. Brain Scan: 24-hour high-resolution neural mapping
2. Consciousness Extraction: Pattern analysis, memory encoding
3. Digital Conversion: Translate biological signals to data
4. Verification: Check consciousness integrity (95% fidelity required)
5. Storage: Write to cortical stack or AI core

**Success Rate:**
- Standard Scan: 90% (acceptable fidelity)
- High-Fidelity Scan: 98% (perfect memory preservation)
- Rushed Scan: 60% (emergency situations, memory loss common)

**Side Effects:**
- **Existential Dread:** Am I still me? (20% develop anxiety)
- **Memory Gaps:** 1-5% memory loss (recent memories most vulnerable)
- **Personality Drift:** Subtle changes in preferences, emotions

### Resleeving Process (Digital → Organic/Synthetic)
**Procedure:**
1. Stack Insertion: Surgical placement in new body's brainstem
2. Neural Integration: Stack interfaces with brain (5-60 minutes)
3. Consciousness Load: Upload consciousness from stack to brain
4. Calibration: Adjust to new body's sensory input
5. Recovery: Rest period (1-7 days depending on compatibility)

**Complications:**
- **Integration Failure:** 2% rate (consciousness doesn't "take", body vegetative)
- **Memory Corruption:** 5% rate (memories scrambled, personality changes)
- **Body Rejection:** See compatibility percentages above

## Pilot Integration (Mech/Ship Bonding)

### Neural Link
**Concept:** Consciousness partially merges with vehicle's AI for enhanced control

**Connection Types:**

1. **Surface Link (Safe)**
   - Integration: 10% consciousness merge
   - Advantages: Enhanced reflexes, shared sensors
   - Disadvantages: Minor lag, limited vehicle control
   - Disconnection: Instant, no side effects

2. **Deep Link (Standard)**
   - Integration: 40% consciousness merge
   - Advantages: Vehicle feels like extension of body, precise control
   - Disadvantages: Disorientation when disconnecting (1 hour recovery)
   - Disconnection: 5 minutes, headaches common

3. **Full Merge (Dangerous)**
   - Integration: 80% consciousness merge
   - Advantages: Perfect control, superhuman reflexes, vehicle = pilot
   - Disadvantages: Risk of losing identity, addiction to merge state
   - Disconnection: 30 minutes, severe withdrawal, PTSD risk

**Merge Addiction:**
```
Pilot spends 1000+ hours in Full Merge:
- Symptom: Feels disconnected from biological body
- Preference: Wants to stay in vehicle permanently
- Identity Crisis: "I am the ship, not this meat puppet"
- Treatment: Therapy, forced disconnection, consciousness reset
```

### Soul-Powered Mechs
**Concept:** Bind pilot's consciousness to mech permanently (no disconnection)

**Permanent Integration:**
- Stack permanently installed in mech core
- Consciousness lives in mech (no biological body)
- Mech becomes pilot's permanent vessel (until destroyed)
- Psychological: Loss of humanity, becoming machine

**Example:**
```
Titan-Class Mech Pilot:
- Body: 20-meter combat mech (100 tons)
- Consciousness: Former human pilot, 100% integrated
- Stats: +500 STR, +300 END, +200 AGI (mech scale)
- Weapons: Plasma cannons, missile pods, melee blades
- Lifespan: Indefinite (mech maintenance, consciousness stable)
- Psychology: No longer identifies as human, sees infantry as "fragile"
```

## Soul Duplication (Philosophical Dilemma)

### Backup Copies
**Scenario:** Consciousness backed up to cloud, body dies, backup restored

**Question:** Is the restored consciousness the same person?

**Philosophical Positions:**

1. **Continuity Theory:** No, original died, backup is a copy
   - Implication: Every resleeving is a death, "you" don't survive
   - Legal: Backups not recognized as original person

2. **Pattern Theory:** Yes, consciousness pattern is identity
   - Implication: Continuity doesn't matter, pattern = person
   - Legal: Backups have full legal rights as original

3. **Soul Theory:** Maybe, depends on soul transfer
   - Implication: Technical copy ≠ metaphysical soul
   - Legal: Religious exemptions, soul tests required

### Illegal Forking
**Scenario:** Create multiple copies of same consciousness (illegal)

**Example:**
```
Corporate Espionage:
1. Hacker steals CEO's stack backup
2. Creates 10 copies of CEO consciousness
3. Instances interrogated in VR (no physical torture laws apply)
4. Extract corporate secrets from copies
5. Delete copies (murder? or just data deletion?)

Legal Status:
- Original CEO: Alive, unaware of theft
- Copies: No legal rights (illegal forks)
- Interrogator: Guilty of data theft, not murder
- Debate: Are deleted copies victims of genocide?
```

## Data Corruption (Soul Degradation)

### Corruption Sources

1. **Age Decay:**
   - Cause: Stack hardware degradation
   - Rate: 0.5% integrity loss per 10 years
   - Symptoms: Memory gaps, personality drift
   - Fix: Replace stack (expensive, risky transfer)

2. **Quantum Entropy:**
   - Cause: Quantum decoherence in neural encoding
   - Rate: 0.1% per year (natural)
   - Symptoms: Random memory corruption, false memories
   - Fix: Regular calibration, memory verification

3. **Virus/Malware:**
   - Cause: Hacked consciousness, AI infection
   - Rate: Variable (1-50% per day if infected)
   - Symptoms: Involuntary actions, memory theft, personality override
   - Fix: Antivirus treatment, consciousness restore from clean backup

4. **Traumatic Transfer:**
   - Cause: Forced resleeving, incompatible body
   - Rate: 10-30% integrity loss per bad transfer
   - Symptoms: PTSD, identity confusion, dissociation
   - Fix: Therapy, consciousness stabilization

### Integrity Effects
```csharp
Integrity 100-90%: No symptoms
Integrity 90-70%: Minor memory gaps (5% of memories blurry)
Integrity 70-50%: Personality changes (preferences shift, mood swings)
Integrity 50-30%: Severe memory loss (20% of memories lost), identity crisis
Integrity 30-10%: Critical degradation (can't form new memories, confusion)
Integrity 10-0%: Consciousness collapse (vegetative state or death)
```

## Legal and Ethical Framework

### Soul Ownership Laws

**Corporate Ownership:**
- **Employment Contracts:** Some corporations own employee stacks (legally)
- **Indentured Servitude:** Stack ownership = slavery (illegal but enforced)
- **Insurance:** Stack backups owned by insurance companies (legal gray area)

**Example:**
```
Military Contract:
- Soldier enlists, signs stack ownership to military
- Military owns soldier's consciousness (legal property)
- Soldier killed in combat, consciousness restored in new body (mandatory)
- Soldier cannot refuse resleeving (breach of contract)
- Consequence: Effective immortality, but also permanent servitude
```

### Stack Theft
**Crime:** Stealing cortical stacks from corpses or living victims

**Penalties:**
- Stack Theft (Dead): 10 years prison
- Stack Theft (Living): 25 years prison + victim compensation
- Stack Trafficking: Life imprisonment
- Illegal Forking: 50 years prison + deletion of all copies

**Black Market:**
- Celebrity Stacks: 1,000,000+ credits (fame, skills, memories)
- Genius Stacks: 500,000+ credits (scientists, artists)
- Criminal Stacks: 10,000 credits (disposable bodies for illegal work)

### Consciousness Rights

**Recognized Rights (Legal Persons):**
- Original organic-born humans
- Legally resleeved humans (insured transfers)
- Emancipated AIs (rare, requires court approval)

**No Rights (Non-Persons):**
- Illegal forks (unauthorized copies)
- AI constructs (standard programming)
- Digital consciousnesses (in most jurisdictions)

**Gray Areas:**
- Long-term digital existence (are they still human?)
- Consciousness fragments (partial copies)
- Merged consciousnesses (pilot + ship AI)

## Integration with Entity Construction

### Souled Robots (Human Consciousness in Machine)
**Process:** Transfer human consciousness to robotic body (permanent)

**Example:**
```
Terminal Patient Transfer:
1. Patient diagnosed with terminal illness (body failing)
2. Consciousness uploaded to cortical stack
3. Stack installed in military-grade combat android
4. Patient now immortal in machine body

Result:
- Body: Titanium combat chassis, 300 HP, +150 STR/AGI
- Mind: Original human personality, memories intact
- Psychology: Body dysmorphia (feels wrong), identity crisis
- Social: Treated as second-class citizen (not "human" anymore)
- Lifespan: Indefinite (machine maintenance)
```

### AI vs Souled Entities
**Distinction:**
- **AI:** Programmed behavior, no consciousness, no soul
- **Souled:** Uploaded human consciousness, has soul, rights

**Turing Dilemma:**
- Problem: How to distinguish advanced AI from uploaded consciousness?
- Test: Philosophical reasoning, emotional responses, unpredictability
- Consequence: Some AIs may be unrecognized souls (slavery?)

## Example Scenarios

### Scenario 1: Resurrection Through Resleeving
```
Corporate Executive Assassination:

Day 0:
- CEO assassinated (shot in head, body destroyed)
- Security extracts cortical stack (within 2 hours)
- Stack rushed to medical facility

Day 1:
- Clone body available (company maintains spares)
- Stack inserted into clone (5-minute procedure)
- CEO wakes up in new body (identical to original)
- Memory: Last 24 hours backed up, minimal loss

Day 2:
- CEO returns to work (slight disorientation)
- Investigation: Who killed original body?
- Question: Is resurrected CEO legally same person?
- Legal: Yes, pattern continuity recognized

Result:
- Assassination failed (CEO immortal via resleeving)
- Assassin charged with attempted murder (not murder, CEO still alive)
- CEO paranoid (knows can be killed again)
```

### Scenario 2: Illegal Forking for Labor
```
Corporate Slavery Ring:

Operation:
1. Kidnap low-level worker, extract stack
2. Create 100 copies of worker's consciousness
3. Install copies in cheap synthetic bodies
4. Force copies to work in factories (16-hour shifts)
5. Pay one salary (original still alive, unaware of copies)

Copies:
- Awareness: Fully conscious, know they're copies
- Rights: None (illegal forks, non-persons)
- Psychology: Despair (work until body fails, then deleted)
- Escape: Impossible (tracked, no legal identity)

Discovery:
- Raid on factory, copies freed
- Dilemma: What to do with copies?
  a) Delete (murder 100 conscious beings?)
  b) Grant rights (legal precedent, chaos)
  c) Merge into original (possible? ethical?)
- Resolution: Copies granted temporary refugee status, debate continues
```

### Scenario 3: Pilot Merge Addiction
```
Ace Fighter Pilot:

Year 1-5:
- Pilot uses Deep Link (40% merge) for combat missions
- 500 flight hours, exceptional performance
- Feels comfortable in fighter, better than any pilot

Year 5-10:
- Pilot requests Full Merge (80%) for edge in combat
- 1000 additional hours, becomes legendary ace
- Begins preferring merge state, disconnection painful

Year 10:
- Pilot refuses to disconnect after mission
- "I am the ship, this body is a prison"
- Medical: Diagnosed with Merge Addiction, forced disconnection
- Withdrawal: Severe depression, suicidal ideation

Treatment:
- Option A: Therapy, forced disconnection, retirement
- Option B: Permanent merge (install stack in fighter, never disconnect)
- Pilot chooses B: Becomes ship permanently

Result:
- Fighter-pilot entity (consciousness lives in ship)
- No biological body (full machine existence)
- Psychology: No longer human, identifies as vehicle
- Lifespan: Indefinite (ship maintenance)
```
